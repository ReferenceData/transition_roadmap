Phase Definitions

E2E     Input 1st
 
Pre-Requisites:  
Pre-Requisites:

Initial Content Population:
 
Country & System Setup and Preparation:
During E2E transitions, countries need to do an initial population of all their content into OGRDS. This is required for IV + Outputs in order to begin building deliverables through CIP. Some key tasks are required before loading to OGRDS. OGRDS will be a slave of legacy IMDB, and legacy will remain the master.     This stage is kicked off with a deep dive of the Input 1st country. Analysis will be done to understand how to properly set up OGRDS as well as the MDL (Massive Data Load) that is used to load Reference Data information into OGRDS. We must prepare the test OGRDS environments to receive this country's data. As many pre-requisites should be completed as possible. Additionally, initial online training sessions should begin.
   
   
   
BAU Incremental Loads:  
Training & UAT:
Once initial content for the country is loaded into OGRDS, we enter an incremental BAU load into OGRDS. Typically loads take place weekly, where any changed or new items since previous load are loaded. This will keep OGRDS as a replica of legacy IMDB. This stage usually takes the longest as it is dependent on completion of all new databases through CIP/eClipse. These will continue until Go Live.     During this stage, 1st load into a test / E2E environment is completed as well as into training environment. The data may not be fully prepared as it will be for go live, but should be sufficient for the requirements for IV to begin their activities. Face to Face (F2F) training will begin during this phase. This initial load will give us a true understand country's status & gaps
   
   
   
Legacy Shutdown:  
Go Live Readiness:
This stage typically begins once we have an input deep dive, where we investigate all the requirements in order to fully shut down legacy environment. This usually begins anywhere between 8-12 months before Go Live. Once we've determined an estimated go live date, this planning will kick off. This runs in parallel to BAU loads, and many times this stage will begin even before all databases are fully live in CIP.     Here the team will continue to push to prepare the country for load into OGRDS Production (live environment). Dry runs will be executed, and all the minimum work required for loading the country to OGRDS must be completed. Data should be as it will be expected for Go Live. Depending on how UAT went before, we will determine if 1 or 2 dry runs are required.
   
   
   
Go Live Readiness:  
Load to Production + Go Live!
This stage is when we really ramp up dedication and focus to go live. This is when training begins, dry run activities occur (as needed), and detailed planning of go live weekend takes place. Depending on the size and complexity of a country, this phase and Legacy shutdown may overlap. This phase typically begins approximately 6 months before go live.     This will be all last requirements needed for go live. We will kick off pre-MDL activities, and begin opening tickets to prepare for Go live weekend. On site support will begin around go live weekend and continue after Go Live. Legacy IMDB will be closed, and load will take place into OGRDS Production. Exceptions will begin to come from SirVal.
